The Gods of Abelon
A reference for Session Zero and beyond
The Eight – Major Gods

The eight major gods exist in four pairs. Each pair governs one aspect of existence. They were people once, long before anyone now living could remember.

The body – its being and its ending
Aeron Life

Life in Abelon is not a gift so much as a responsibility, and those who feel that most keenly tend to find their way to Aeron. The belief is practical rather than spiritual: something alive deserves to be tended, protected, not wasted. You see it in how his followers work rather than how they pray.

Zareth Decay

Most people respect Zareth without thinking much about it, because the evidence is everywhere. The harvest rots and feeds the soil. The old tree falls and the young one grows through it. His followers understand that endings are part of the work rather than the enemy of it, and they do not look away from the dying or the dead.

The soul – its journey and its rest
Maelor The soul’s journey

Maelor governs the self that knows it exists: purpose, consciousness, the feeling that a life has a shape worth finding. His followers tend to be people who cannot stop asking why they are here, monks, philosophers, those who have come close to death and returned changed. To follow Maelor is to take your own story seriously enough to look for its meaning.

Kazimir The soul’s sealing

When a life ends, Kazimir is the one who closes the book, not with grief but with care. He governs memory and preservation, the keeping of what a person was, and his followers are those who understand that the back page matters as much as the first. Historians follow him, as do those who tend graves, carry oral traditions, and refuse to let someone be forgotten.

The heart – its balance and its beating
Avelyn Harmony

Avelyn governs the bonds between people, the quiet presence of connection when it is working as it should. She is worshipped in the moments of stillness that come after something has been built together: a marriage settled into, a community that has learned to hold. Her followers are not people who seek peace so much as people who understand what it takes to keep it.

Varkov Strife

Varkov has few formal followers but almost everyone has called on him at some point, in the moment before a fight or a hard negotiation or the argument that finally says the thing that needed saying. Strife in Abelon carries no shame. It is what happens when something matters enough to push against.

The mind – its reason and its will
Talwyn Reason

Talwyn’s shrines appear in libraries and courts and the rooms where consequential decisions get made, which tells you something about who follows him. The belief is not that thinking is virtuous in itself but that a wrong conclusion kills, and careful people understand this. His followers take time when others rush, and they are usually right to.

Sovan Will

Sovan governs the spark, the impulse, the moment that arrives without announcement and asks to be acted on. There is no doctrine and no deliberation here, only the question of whether you trust what moves in you enough to move with it. His followers are people who have learned to answer that question without hesitating.

The Many – Minor Gods

The minor gods are more recent, more numerous, and more varied. Each began as a person whose legacy inspired enough devotion to sustain them beyond death. They are still recognisably themselves, though belief shapes them slowly over time.
